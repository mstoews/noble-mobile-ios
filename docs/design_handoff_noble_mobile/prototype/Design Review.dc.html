Noble Ledger Mobile — design review
SwiftUI app · 8 tabs + login + AI chat · reviewed against the NobleLedger design system
HIGH
1 · The app doesn't wear the brand. Login, the More header and the chat FAB all use an ad-hoc navy gradient (rgb(36,51,92) → rgb(51,102,179)); the More grid assigns six unrelated tints to its cards. NobleLedger's identity — emerald-700 primary, slate neutrals, the bronze crown mark (always shown inside a framed container) — appears nowhere. Every redesign below moves onto the emerald/slate system.
HIGH
2 · Navigation buries the daily work. Ten destinations sit behind 5 tabs + a More grid. The two things a treasurer opens the app for — signing off payments and booking journals — are generic cards on the second screen, with no counts. Concepts also overlap: "Invoices" is really AP bill capture, "Journals" and "Journal Booking" are two entries to one job, and "Payables" exists both as a dashboard widget and a More card. The floating chat button overlaps the tab bar and list content on every screen. See 1b, 1c, 1d.
HIGH
3 · Login front-loads friction. Three required fields including "Company ID" — jargon users won't have at hand, and it also gates Sign in with Apple (explained only in a caption). The disabled button renders near-white on navy and reads as broken; stock white roundedBorder fields and small red error text sit harshly on the dark gradient. There's no Face ID affordance at login and the company is never remembered. See 1e, 1f.
MED
4 · The dashboard has no hierarchy. Seven co-equal material cards stack forever: the headline numbers are .subheadline-sized while section labels shout, the four summary tiles use four arbitrary colors, and raw signed balances render liabilities red as if something is wrong. AR and AP repeat the same aging widget back-to-back. The DS pattern is the opposite: numbers are the hero, and green/red is reserved as a "check-engine light". See 1g.
MED
5 · Chat "quick actions" are destructive bulk operations. The assistant's empty state offers "Close all open journals" and "Book all open journals" as one-tap suggestions — irreversible ledger mutations styled like conversation starters, with no counts and no confirmation framing. See 1h.
LOW
6 · Smaller fixes. Journal rows carry up to five chips/metadata fragments each — cap at status + one fact. Status colors (orange/blue/green) should map to DS tokens. "Due 7d (2)" → "Due this week". The Invoices bottom toolbar duplicates what a segmented control or nav push does natively. Good things to keep: monospaced digits everywhere, pull-to-refresh, confirmation dialogs on destructive actions, and the visible version string.
